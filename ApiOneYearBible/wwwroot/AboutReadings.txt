The readings are accessed by clicking a calendar date. If you want to start on
January 1st, navigate with the back/next buttons to that date.

<p>
The old testament comprises about 75% of the Christian bible. So, readings from there
will take up the most time. If you want, you can maybe read the old testament readings
one year, and read the new testament readings along with the psalm and proverbs readings
another year. Alternatively, another strategy could be to the read the new testament
readings in the morning, and read the old testament readings in the evening.
</p>
<p>
Why are Psalm readings separate from the old testament readings, and why are they important?
There are 150 Psalms in the old testament. Therefore, you'll be reading them twice in a year;
one per day. King David, the one who defeated Goliath, is credited with writing 73 or 75 of
the 150 Psalms in the Book of Psalms. His psalms are often songs of praise, prayers for help,
and expressions of repentance. In short, they can show us how to communicate with God. Psalm
23 is probably the most widely known. My mom would say that Psalms 27, 34 and 37 are gold mine
chapters.
</p>
<p>
Why are Proverbs readings separate from the old testament readings, and why are they important?
The daily readings only comprise a few verses each day perhaps so one can perhaps ruminate on
its teaching. They offer advice to make life run more smoothly navigating complex situations with
divine insight. The Proverbs also offer moral instruction, relationship management, understanding
God, and correction and growth. In short, they can serve as a poetic roadmap for righteous living,
teaching that true success comes from following God's wisdom.
</p>
